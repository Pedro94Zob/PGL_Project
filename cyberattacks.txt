					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><p>
A <a href="/wiki/Cyberattack" title="Cyberattack">cyberattack</a> is any type of offensive maneuver employed by individuals or whole organizations that targets computer information systems, infrastructures, computer networks, and/or personal computer devices by various means of malicious acts usually originating from an anonymous source that either steals, alters, or destroys a specified target by hacking into a susceptible system.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Indiscriminate_attacks">Indiscriminate attacks</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Indiscriminate attacks"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>These attacks are wide-ranging, global and do not seem to discriminate among governments and companies.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Operation_Shady_RAT" title="Operation Shady RAT">Operation Shady RAT</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/World_of_Hell" title="World of Hell">World of Hell</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Red_October_(malware)" title="Red October (malware)">Red October</a>, discovered in 2012, was reportedly operating worldwide for up to five years prior to discovery, transmitting information ranging from diplomatic secrets to personal information, including from mobile devices.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/WannaCry_ransomware_attack" title="WannaCry ransomware attack">WannaCry ransomware attack</a> on 12 May 2017 affected hundreds of thousands of computers in more than 150 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2017_Petya_cyberattack" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 Petya cyberattack">2017 Petya cyberattack</a></li></ul>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Destructive_attacks">Destructive attacks</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destructive attacks"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>These attacks relate to inflicting damage on specific organizations.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Great_Hacker_War" title="Great Hacker War">Great Hacker War</a>, and purported "gang war" in <a href="/wiki/Cyberspace" title="Cyberspace">cyberspace</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LulzRaft" title="LulzRaft">LulzRaft</a>, a hacker group known for a low-impact attack in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Operation_Ababil" title="Operation Ababil">Operation Ababil</a>, conducted against <a href="/wiki/Banking_in_the_United_States" title="Banking in the United States">American financial institutions</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/TV5Monde#April_2015_cyberattack_and_resulting_disruption" title="TV5Monde">TV5Monde April 2015 cyberattack</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vulcanbot" title="Vulcanbot">Vulcanbot</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shamoon" title="Shamoon">Shamoon</a>, a modular computer virus, was used in 2012 in an attack on 30,000 <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Aramco" title="Saudi Aramco">Saudi Aramco</a> workstations, causing the company to spend a week restoring their services.<sup id="cite_ref-Iran_Malware_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iran_Malware-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>

It was designed to sabotage <a href="/wiki/Iran%27s_nuclear_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Iran&#39;s nuclear program">Iran's nuclear program</a> with what would seem like a long series of unfortunate accidents.</li>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Cyberwarfare">Cyberwarfare</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cyberwarfare"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Cyberwarfare" title="Cyberwarfare">Cyberwarfare</a></div>
<p>These are politically motivated destructive attacks aimed at sabotage and espionage.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2007_cyberattacks_on_Estonia" title="2007 cyberattacks on Estonia">2007 cyberattacks on Estonia</a>, wide-ranging attacks targeting government and commercial institutions</li>
<li>2008 <a href="/wiki/Cyberattacks_during_the_Russo-Georgian_War" title="Cyberattacks during the Russo-Georgian War">Cyberattacks during the Russo-Georgian War</a>, a series of cyberattacks that swamped and disabled websites of numerous South Ossetian, Georgian, Russian, and Azerbaijani organizations. The attacks were initiated three weeks before the shooting war began in what is regarded as "the first case in the history of a coordinated cyberspace domain attack synchronized with major combat actions in the other warfighting domains (consisting of Land, Air, Sea, and Space)."</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2009_DDoS_attacks_against_South_Korea" title="2009 DDoS attacks against South Korea">2009 DDoS attacks against South Korea</a>, a series of coordinated cyberattacks against major government, news media, and financial websites in South Korea and the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_2009_cyberattacks" class="mw-redirect" title="July 2009 cyberattacks">July 2009 cyberattacks</a>, against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and the United States</li>
<li>2009 <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Network" title="Shadow Network">Shadow Network</a>, a China-based computer espionage operation that stole classified documents and emails from the Indian government, the office of the Dalai Lama, and other high-level government networks.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2010_Australian_cyberattacks" title="February 2010 Australian cyberattacks">2010 Australian cyberattacks</a>, a series of denial-of-service attacks conducted by the Anonymous online community against the Australian government in response to proposed web censorship regulations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010_cyberattacks_on_Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 cyberattacks on Burma">2010 cyberattacks on Burma</a>, related to the <a href="/wiki/2010_Myanmar_general_election" title="2010 Myanmar general election">2010 Myanmar general election</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010_cyberattacks_on_Myanmar" title="2010 cyberattacks on Myanmar">2010 cyberattacks on Myanmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denial-of-service_attack#Distributed_attack" title="Denial-of-service attack">distributed</a> <a href="/wiki/Denial-of-service_attack" title="Denial-of-service attack">denial-of-service attacks</a> (DDoS) ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Myanmar_general_election" title="2010 Myanmar general election">2010 Myanmar general election</a>, which is widely viewed as a <a href="/wiki/Sham_election" class="mw-redirect" title="Sham election">sham election</a>.</li>
<li>2010 <a href="/wiki/Operation_Olympic_Games" title="Operation Olympic Games">Operation Olympic Games</a>, against Iranian nuclear facilities, allegedly conducted by the United States</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010_Japan%E2%80%93South_Korea_cyberwarfare" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Japan–South Korea cyberwarfare">2010 Japan–South Korea cyberwarfare</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_government_hackings" title="2011 Canadian government hackings">2011 Canadian government hackings</a>, hackers using IP addresses from China infiltrated 3 departments within the government and exfiltrated classified data. The attacks resulted in the government cutting off internet access in the departments affected and various responses from both the Canadian government and the Chinese government.</li>
<li>2012 <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ababil" title="Operation Ababil">Operation Ababil</a>, a series of cyber attacks starting in 2012, targeting various American financial institutions and carried out by a group calling itself the Cyber fighters of Izz Ad-Din Al Qassam.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2013_Singapore_cyberattacks" title="2013 Singapore cyberattacks">2013 Singapore cyberattacks</a>, attack by <a href="/wiki/Anonymous_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Anonymous (group)">Anonymous</a> "in response to <a href="/wiki/Internet_censorship_in_Singapore" title="Internet censorship in Singapore">web censorship regulations in the country</a>, specifically on <a href="/wiki/News_media" title="News media">news outlets</a>"</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2013_South_Korea_cyberattack" title="2013 South Korea cyberattack">2013 South Korea cyberattack</a>, two major sets of cyberattacks on South Korean targets attributed to elements within North Korea.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Office_of_Personnel_Management_data_breach" title="Office of Personnel Management data breach">Office of Personnel Management data breach</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2015_Ukraine_power_grid_hack" title="2015 Ukraine power grid hack">2015 Ukraine power grid hack</a>, took place during an ongoing conflict in Ukraine and is attributed to a Russian advanced persistent threat group known as "Sandworm". It is the first publicly acknowledged successful cyberattack on a power grid.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2016_Kyiv_cyberattack" title="2016 Kyiv cyberattack">2016 Kyiv cyberattack</a>, which caused another power outage</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Committee_cyber_attacks" title="Democratic National Committee cyber attacks">Democratic National Committee cyber attacks</a>, against the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Committee" title="Democratic National Committee">Democratic National Committee</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a>-sponsored <a href="/wiki/Cyber_spying" title="Cyber spying">cyber-espionage</a> groups <a href="/wiki/Cozy_Bear" title="Cozy Bear">Cozy Bear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fancy_Bear" title="Fancy Bear">Fancy Bear</a>, possibly to assist <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump's</a> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump_2016_presidential_campaign" title="Donald Trump 2016 presidential campaign">2016 presidential campaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2017_cyberattacks_on_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 cyberattacks on Ukraine">2017 cyberattacks on Ukraine</a>, A series of powerful cyberattacks using the Petya malware began on 27 June 2017 that swamped websites of Ukrainian organizations, including banks, ministries, newspapers and electricity firms. Similar infections were reported in France, Germany, Italy, Poland, Russia, United Kingdom, the United States and Australia.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2019_cyberattacks_on_Sri_Lanka" title="2019 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka">2019 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka</a>, The 2019 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka were a series of powerful cyberattacks on at least 10 Sri Lankan domestic websites with the public domains of .lk and .com.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2020_cyberattacks_on_Sri_Lanka" title="2020 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka">2020 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka</a>, a series of cyberattacks on at least 5 Sri Lankan national websites with the top-level domains of .gov and .com.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2021_Cyberattacks_on_Sri_Lanka" class="mw-redirect" title="2021 Cyberattacks on Sri Lanka">2021 Cyberattacks on Sri Lanka</a>,  series of cyberattacks on at least 10 Sri Lankan national websites including Google.lk domain</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/OpIsrael" title="OpIsrael">#OpIsrael</a>, a broad "<a href="/wiki/Anti-Zionism" title="Anti-Zionism">anti-Israel</a>" attack</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2022_Ukraine_cyberattacks" title="2022 Ukraine cyberattacks">2022 Ukraine cyberattacks</a>, undertaken during the prelude to the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2022_cyberattacks_on_Romania" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 cyberattacks on Romania">2022 cyberattacks on Romania</a>, which occurred after a visit of Romanian officials to Kyiv where more support against Russia was promised while the invasion was taking place</li>
<li>2023 Cyberattack on Australia, under which  the IRGC launched a cyberattack against an Australian organization to obtain data from an extortion and double extortion ransomware operation. Australia expressed deep concern over IRGC's interference, including online harassment of Australian citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Stolen_e-mail_addresses_and_login_credentials">Stolen e-mail addresses and login credentials</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Stolen e-mail addresses and login credentials"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>

</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/RockYou" title="RockYou">RockYou</a> – in 2009, the company experienced a data breach resulting in the exposure of over 32 million user accounts.</li>
<li>Vestige (online store) – in 2010, a band of anonymous hackers has rooted the servers of the site and leaked half a gigabyte's worth of its private data.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> – in 2012, hackers posted login credentials for more than 453,000 user accounts,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> doing so again in January 2013<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and in January 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adobe_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe Systems">Adobe</a> – in 2013, hackers obtained access to Adobe's networks and stole user information and downloaded the <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> for some of Adobe programs.<sup id="cite_ref-bighack_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bighack-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It attacked 150 million customers.<sup id="cite_ref-bighack_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bighack-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LivingSocial" title="LivingSocial">LivingSocial</a> – in 2013, the company suffered a security breach that has exposed names, e-mail addresses and password data for up to 50 million of its users.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> – in March 2020, hackers leaked information on login credentials from the staff members at WHO.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In response to cyberattacks, they stated that “Ensuring the security of health information for Member States and the privacy of users interacting with us a priority for WHO at all times, but also particularly during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.”<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2017_Equifax_data_breach" title="2017 Equifax data breach">2017 Equifax data breach</a> – In 2017, <a href="/wiki/Equifax" title="Equifax">Equifax Inc</a>. announced that a cyber-security breach occurred between May and mid July of that year. <a href="/wiki/Cyber_criminals" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyber criminals">Cyber criminals</a> had accessed approximately 145.5 million U.S. Equifax consumers' personal data, including their full names, <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_number" title="Social Security number">Social Security numbers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Credit_card" title="Credit card">credit card</a> information, <a href="/wiki/Birthday" title="Birthday">birth dates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Address" title="Address">addresses</a>, and, in some cases, <a href="/wiki/Driver%27s_license" title="Driver&#39;s license">driver's license</a> numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Industries" title="Goodwill Industries">Goodwill Industries</a> – in September 2014, the company suffered from a credit card data breach that affected the charitable retailer's stores in at least 21 states. Another two retailers were affected.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Home_Depot" title="Home Depot">Home Depot</a> – in September 2014, the cybercriminals that compromised Home Depot's network and installed malware on the home-supply company's <a href="/wiki/Point_of_sale" title="Point of sale">point-of-sale systems</a> likely stole information on 56 million <a href="/wiki/Payment_card" title="Payment card">payment cards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>StarDust – in 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Botnet" title="Botnet">botnet</a> compromised 20,000 cards in active campaign hitting US merchants.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a> – in 2013, approximately 40 million credit and debit card accounts were impacted in a credit card breach.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> According to another estimate, it compromised as many as 110 million Target customers.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Visa_Inc." title="Visa Inc.">Visa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a> – in 2012, they warned card-issuing banks that a third-party payments processor suffered a security breach, affecting up to 10 million credit cards.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Subway_(restaurant)" title="Subway (restaurant)">Subway</a> – in 2012, two <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> men admitted to participating in an international conspiracy that hacked into credit-card payment terminals at more than 150 Subway <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">restaurant franchises</a> and stole data for more than 146,000 accounts.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/MasterCard" class="mw-redirect" title="MasterCard">MasterCard</a> – in 2005, the company announced that up to 40 million cardholders may have had account information stolen due to one of its payment processors being hacked.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/IRLeaks_attack_on_Iranian_banks" title="IRLeaks attack on Iranian banks">IRLeaks attack on Iranian banks</a></li></ul>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Blockchain_and_cryptocurrencies">Blockchain and cryptocurrencies</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Blockchain and cryptocurrencies"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<ul><li>2014 <a href="/wiki/Mt._Gox" title="Mt. Gox">Mt. Gox</a> exchange exploits</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_DAO_(organization)" class="mw-redirect" title="The DAO (organization)">The DAO</a> fork – in June 2016, users exploited a vulnerability in The DAO, a <a href="/wiki/Decentralized_autonomous_organization" title="Decentralized autonomous organization">decentralized autonomous organization</a> formed as a venture capital fund, to siphon a third of the fund's ether (about $50 million at the time of the hack).<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Poly_Network_exploit" title="Poly Network exploit">Poly Network exploit</a> – in August 2021, anonymous hackers transferred over $610 million in cryptocurrencies to external wallets. Although it was one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Decentralized_finance" title="Decentralized finance">DeFi</a> hacks ever, all assets were eventually returned over the following two weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Wormhole hack – in early February 2022, an unknown hacker exploited a vulnerability on the DeFi platform Wormhole, making off with $320 million in wrapped ether.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Ronin Network hack – in March 2022, North Korean state-sponsored <a href="/wiki/Lazarus_Group" title="Lazarus Group">Lazarus Group</a> used hacked private keys to withdraw $625 million in ether and USDC from the Ronin bridge,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> an Ethereum sidechain built for the <a href="/wiki/Non-fungible_token" title="Non-fungible token">NFT-based</a> video game <a href="/wiki/Axie_Infinity" title="Axie Infinity">Axie Infinity</a>.</li>
<li>Nomad bridge hack – in early August 2022, hackers targeted a misconfigured <a href="/wiki/Smart_contract" title="Smart contract">smart contract</a> in a "free-for-all" attack,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> withdrawing nearly $200 million in cryptocurrencies from the Nomad cross-chain bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>52<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>The Uncle Maker attack – an attack on <a href="/wiki/Ethereum" title="Ethereum">Ethereum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/F2Pool" class="mw-redirect" title="F2Pool">F2Pool</a> <a href="/wiki/Mining_pool" title="Mining pool">mining pool</a>, which lasted between 2020 and 2022, but was only discovered in 2022 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aviv_Yaish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aviv Yaish (page does not exist)">Aviv Yaish</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilad_Stern&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gilad Stern (page does not exist)">Gilad Stern</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aviv_Zohar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aviv Zohar (page does not exist)">Aviv Zohar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>53<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>54<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>BNB Chain hack – in early October 2022, about $570 million in cryptocurrency was stolen from a bridge for the BNB Chain, a blockchain operated by the <a href="/wiki/Binance" title="Binance">Binance</a> exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>55<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Because a majority of the tokens could not be transferred off-chain, the hacker ultimately made off with about $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>56<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Stolen_medical-related_data">Stolen medical-related data</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Stolen medical-related data"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<ul><li>By May, three healthcare payer organizations had been attacked in the United States in 2014 and 2015: <a href="/wiki/Anthem_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Anthem Inc.">Anthem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Premera_Blue_Cross" title="Premera Blue Cross">Premera Blue Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blue_Cross_Blue_Shield_Association#CareFirst" title="Blue Cross Blue Shield Association">CareFirst</a>. The three attacks together netted information on more than 91 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-dance2015_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dance2015-57"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In January 2022, the <a href="/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross" title="International Committee of the Red Cross">International Committee of the Red Cross</a> made a public plea to hackers who had <a href="/wiki/Red_Cross_cyberattack" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Cross cyberattack">attacked the organisation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tj-red-cross-appeals_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tj-red-cross-appeals-58"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>58<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>
